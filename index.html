Exoponential Growth

Industrialization has allowed our species to grow to staggering numbers. The human population has grown seven-fold since the beginning of the 19th century. In fact, all the people living today make up 6.5% of all people who have ever lived. We have grown exponentially, inhabiting virtually all livable land on the planet.

Density and Urbanization

The truth is that our exponential growth rate has stopped in modern times. Growth rates are down in many parts of the developed world. However, some areas are still expected to show huge population booms. These three contries: India, China, and Nigeria together are expected to produce 35% of total population growth in the next 30 years.

This growth will mostly occur in dense urban areas. The UN projects that 68% of the population will live in cities by 2050? This massive movement of urbanization creates many lagistical problems to be solved. What implications do increased population growth and urbanization have on the earth's carrying capacity?

Carrying Capacity

Many scientists believe we are reaching the limits of sustainable growth rates on earth. The physics of supplying proper food, clothing, shelter and power to the addition of billions of more people on earth has limitations. These limitations will soon be stretched as the human population grows to 10 billion in 2050. It is also projected that we will need the equivalent of 3 earths to support us by 2050.

The ecological footprint of our species is causing significant disruption to the natural biotic functions of our environment. The need for more agriculture to feed our growing numbers is resulting is mass deforestation and loss of ecosystems.

Sustainable Future

Sustainability is the key. Here are some solutions to help mitigate the effects of population growth:

  • Focus on sustainable urban development with renewable energy
  • Education and initiatives addressing population growth and consumption habits
  • Stop agricultural expansion and focus on growing more on smaller plots of land
  • Use our resources more efficiently
  • Shift diets
  • Reduce our waste
Overall, the world will need to come together to overcome the challenges of an increasing human population closer to carrying capacity. Many solutions will require a shift in thinking and policy. However, since there are no more planets for us to go to, we must take on the responsibility of providing a quality standard of living for all people on Earth.
